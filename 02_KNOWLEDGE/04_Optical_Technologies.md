# Optical Technologies

## 1. Purpose

Provide stable technical foundations for PON generations, ODN, optical power, coexistence and evolution.

## 2. Technology Families

- EPON/10G-EPON;
- GPON;
- XG-PON and XGS-PON;
- Combo PON;
- 50G PON and future coexistence.

## 3. Engineering Dimensions

For each technology evaluate downstream/upstream rates, wavelength plan, coexistence, optical classes, reach, split ratio, ONU ecosystem, power/heat, cost and operational maturity.

## 4. ODN Fundamentals

ODN is passive but operationally critical. Model feeder, distribution, drop, ODF, closures, connectors, splices and splitters. Optical faults often arise from cleanliness, bending, water ingress, poor splices, overloaded topology or inaccurate records.

## 5. Optical Power

Do not use one universal dBm threshold across all technologies and classes. Evaluate the exact module/ONU specification, total budget, margin, history and peer distribution.

## 6. Evolution Principle

Choose technology by demand, economics, ecosystem and operations—not maximum nominal speed. For many small ISPs, GPON remains the cost-performance baseline; XGS-PON is the main upgrade path; 50G PON is selective until ecosystem and economics mature.

## 7. Digital Twin Link

A useful optical twin includes wavelength/technology, optical class, link budget, measured power, expected power, degradation trend, splitter topology and affected services.

## 8. Freshness

Current standards, modules, vendor boards and commercial deployment status require web/official-document verification.
