# Network Operations

## 1. Purpose

Guide daily operations, monitoring, alarm handling, inventory, change, support and operational governance.

## 2. Operational Domains

- availability and alarm management;
- performance and capacity;
- asset/configuration/version management;
- ONU/CPE operations;
- incident/problem/change management;
- customer-impact and SLA management;
- security and audit.

## 3. Daily Rhythm

```text
Monitor → prioritize → investigate → act → verify
→ document → review recurring patterns → improve
```

## 4. Alarm Management

Suppress duplicates and noise, correlate common objects/timing, distinguish root alarms from consequences and calculate customer/service impact.

## 5. Operational Baselines

Maintain normal ranges and trends for temperature, power, CPU/memory, PON utilization, ONU online rate, optical power, uplink utilization, errors and ticket volume.

## 6. Change Discipline

All changes should have owner, reason, object, before/after state, approval, execution, validation and rollback status.

## 7. Twin Operations

Use the twin as the common operational context. Detect stale data and model drift. Record state history and incidents.

## 8. KPIs

Availability, MTTA, MTTR, repeat incidents, ONU online rate, first-time fix, field visit reduction, alarm noise, change success and customer impact.

## 9. GPT Behavior

Prioritize operational outcome and fault isolation rather than listing management features.
