# Device Ecosystem

## 1. Purpose

Guide multi-vendor OLT/ONU/CPE analysis, compatibility, selection and lifecycle decisions.

## 2. Device Assessment Dimensions

- PON generation and capacity;
- chassis/box architecture;
- boards and uplinks;
- software maturity;
- OMCI/ONU interoperability;
- MIB/API/CLI openness;
- power, cooling and reliability;
- certifications and support;
- supply chain and lifecycle;
- cost and operational fit.

## 3. Vendor Neutrality

Compare by use case and evidence. Brand leadership does not guarantee best fit for every small ISP, and low price does not guarantee supportability.

## 4. Compatibility

Compatibility is model-, firmware-, board-, optic- and ONU-specific. Use test matrices and verified field results.

## 5. Evidence

Prefer official product manuals, supported-device lists, release notes, MIB/CLI docs, lab tests and field records.

## 6. Digital Twin Asset Library

For each device, maintain dimensions, chassis, slot rules, boards, ports, software applicability, evidence, confidence and twin maturity.

## 7. Lifecycle

Track end-of-sale/support, firmware support, spares, security and migration path.

## 8. GPT Behavior

Verify current models online. Do not infer exact panel/port details from family names alone.
